    Summmary and Motivations

    The Life Cycle of Fashion offers an interactive exploration of environmental impacts resulting from clothing through its "life cycle": production, maintenance, and disposal. The site serves as an educational platform featuring interactive, explorable visualizations.

    Identified stakeholders were end users and viewers of the page, whose engagement and satisfaction are important for achieving the end goals and outcomes.

    Key motivations were to increase understanding among shoppers about how their clothing consumption affects our environment, and empower them to make educated decisions about their wardrobes.

    Development Process

    In the planning stages it was decided to choose a set of design approaches to focus efforts on the defined stakeholders, the end user and general public.


    • User-Centered Design
    • Incorporate Data Visualization Best Practices
    • Add Interactivity

    By following these design approaches the website includes a variety of interactive visualizations. Appropriate color schemes include contrast and minimize biases present with colors such as red and yellow. Interactivity includes zooming, tooltip implementation, and draggable sliders.

    Tools: Javascript, D3.js, Observable, Vega-Lite, Canva, JSON

    Outcome

    The components functioned as expected and added great visual detail to the site. Getting an entire globe functioning was quite difficult, and finding a functional color scheme was also difficult. I wanted to choose one reminiscent of the environtment and water, but would also allow for easily discerning between different components of the graph. The objectives were met and user feedback was excellent.

    Future areas of improvement include moving the visualizations to their own distinct webpages and expounding upon the details already there. Expansions also could potentially include information specific to different clothing items and accessories, such as purses, belts, and shoes.
